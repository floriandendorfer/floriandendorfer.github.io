Work in Progress

Platform Entry And Self-Preferencing
Abstract Vertically integrated platforms allegedly favor their own first-party (1P) products over third-party (3P) ones. I build a model to analyze the incentive of a gatekeeper platform to sell a 1P product (platform entry) and engage in self-preferencing. Further, I investigate the implications this has for 3P sellers and consumers. In my model, self-preferencing shields the 1P product from competition and permits the platform to set a relatively high price. The platform is also less exposed to the double marginalization problem if it sells the 1P product. It charges 3P sellers a comparatively lower commission fee, which is partly passed on to consumers. I find that, under fairly general distributional assumptions, the commission fee reduction causes more consumers to enter the marketplace. The platform, 3P sellers, and consumers are all better off if there is a 1P product, self-preferencing notwithstanding.
